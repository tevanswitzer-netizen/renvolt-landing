## PART I: THE HERO CAR

In 1971, Porsche took their most beloved sports car — the 911 — stripped it down, lifted it up, and entered it in the most hostile motorsport event on Earth: The East African Safari Rally.

Dirt roads. Monsoon mud. 5,000 kilometres of punishing African terrain.

The car finished. Battered, dusty, and completely alive.

Then it disappeared into the African bush for two decades. Forgotten. Left for dead.

It survived.

When they found it again — sun-bleached and abandoned — the engine still turned. The spirit of the car had never quit.

**That car is Number #11. The Yellow Safari 911.**

And it is the soul of Renvolt.

## PART II: THE CAMPAIGN CONCEPT

**Campaign Name:** *"SAFARI REBORN"*
**Tagline:** *"The wilderness didn't kill it. Gasoline might have. We won't let that happen."*

**The Mission:**
Renvolt will commission a full, faithful restoration and electric conversion of a 1971 Porsche 911 Safari — the "Safari Reborn." This will be the hero vehicle for a new annual EV off-road rally event: **The Renvolt Safari Rally**, staged across Alberta's most unforgiving wilderness corridors.

This is not a car show. This is not a corporate sponsorship. This is a **declaration of what Alberta's energy future looks like.**

## PART IV: THE EVENT CONCEPT

### "The Renvolt Safari Rally — Alberta Edition"

*An annual EV off-road rally staged on Alberta's most brutal wilderness routes.*

**Format:**
The rally is NOT a circuit race. It is a point-to-point endurance event across Alberta's secondary highway and gravel road network — deliberately chosen to follow Renvolt's own charging corridor loop.

**The Route:**
Bear Hills Casino → Ponoka → Drayton Valley → Slave Lake → Lac La Biche → Back to Bear Hills.
(The Renvolt Loop. 600 km of gravel, asphalt, and everything in between.)

**The Rules:**

* Zero emission vehicles only. No exceptions.
* Must be road-capable (no purpose-built race machines). Real production cars, real conversions.
* The hero vehicle: **Safari Reborn** — the Renvolt-built electric 911.

**The Point:**
Every single charging stop on the rally route is a Renvolt hub. The route *proves* the network exists and works. Every EV that completes the loop proves that the infrastructure is ready.

This is not a marketing stunt. This is a **live demonstration of Alberta's electric future**, disguised as the most exciting motor event in Canadian history.

**Brand Experience at Each Stop:**
At every Renvolt hub on the route, there is a live event: local food, music, community outreach, and live charging of the Safari Reborn vehicle on Renvolt equipment. The Indigenous community partners (Louis Bull Tribe, Sawridge, Beaver Lake Cree) are featured as the hosts — because this corridor was built *with* them, *for* them.

I have a confession.

I fell in love with a car I never owned, from a race I never watched, on a continent I've never been to.

In 1971, Porsche built a 911 and entered it in the East African Safari Rally. Five thousand kilometres of African road. Monsoon mud. Heat. Gravel. All of it.

The car finished.

When they found it, the engine still turned.
