2. Industry Overview
====================

Canada’s EV Market
------------------

Canada’s federal government has mandated that 100% of new light-duty vehicle sales must be zero-emission by 2035. This creates a structural, policy-driven demand for EV charging infrastructure independent of market sentiment.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Metric** | **Data Point** |
| Canada EV registrations (2025) | ~250,000 cumulative; ~13% of new sales |
| Alberta EV registrations | ~12,000–15,000 cumulative |
| Alberta annual EV growth | 15–20% per year |
| Federal ZEV mandate | 100% of new sales by 2035 |
| Public chargers in Alberta | ~600 (Level 2 + DC combined) |
| DC fast chargers in Alberta | ~120–150 |
| IEA recommended ratio | 1 charger per 10 EVs |
| Alberta’s actual DC fast ratio | ~1 per 100 EVs |

The Infrastructure Gap
----------------------

Alberta has approximately one DC fast charger for every 100 registered EVs—ten times below the IEA’s recommended ratio. The most critical gap is along the QE2 Highway between Leduc/Edmonton and Red Deer: approximately 100 km without a single DC fast charger on the province’s busiest corridor.

The Demand Charge Problem
-------------------------

Demand charges—utility fees based on peak power draw, not total energy consumed—represent 30–50% of operating costs for DC fast-charging sites. This is the primary reason many charging sites are unprofitable. Renvolt solves this with a co-located battery that shaves peak demand, reducing demand charges by 50–70% and ensuring long-term viability without ongoing government support.

Strategic Advantages

### 100 km DC Fast-Charging Gap on QE2

There is no DC fast charger at Bear Hills or anywhere on approximately 100 km of the QE2 between Leduc/Edmonton (north) and Red Deer (south). This is the single longest gap in DC fast-charging coverage on Alberta’s busiest highway.

### Why EVs Will Stop at Bear Hills

• Winter range loss: EVs lose 30–50% range at -25°C. A Tesla Model 3 drops from 438 km to ~220 km rated range. Edmonton to Calgary is 300 km — most EVs cannot complete the trip in winter without charging.

• Highway traffic: 35,000–50,000 vehicles/day pass on QE2 at this location

• Natural rest stop: Casino guests stay 2–6 hours; Petro-Can attracts existing highway traffic

• EV drivers plan stops: Charger apps (PlugShare, ABRP) will route drivers to Bear Hills as the only option for 100 km

• Captive foot traffic: 20–40 min charging sessions create in-store spending at casino, restaurant, and convenience store

### Traffic Directionality

Bear Hills Casino is accessed via an interchange near Highway 611, serving both northbound and southbound QE2 traffic. Northbound drivers (Calgary → Edmonton) have direct access; southbound drivers access via the interchange with ~1–2 minutes additional travel. Approximately 70–80% of charging demand is expected from northbound traffic, with 20–30% from southbound drivers who require a charge and have no alternative for 100 km. Highway signage on both sides of the QE2 will maximize southbound awareness.

5. Battery Sizing & Optimization
================================

Battery Functions
-----------------

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Function** | **How It Works** |
| Demand Charge Shaving | Battery absorbs peak loads so utility sees a lower, flatter demand profile — reducing demand charges by 50–70% |
| Energy Arbitrage | Charge overnight at off-peak rates ($0.05–0.08/kWh); discharge during peak ($0.12–0.18/kWh) |
| Grid Services | Utility pays Renvolt to discharge battery during grid stress (frequency regulation, demand response) |
| Charging Continuity | Ensures uninterrupted sessions during momentary grid fluctuations |
