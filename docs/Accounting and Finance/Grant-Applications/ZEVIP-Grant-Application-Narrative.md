# ZEVIP Project Narrative: The Renvolt Bear Hills Pilot

**Applicant Name:** Renvolt
**Project Title:** QE2 Indigenous Corridor DC Fast Charging Pilot
**Proposed Site:** Bear Hills Casino & Travel Resort (Maskwacis, AB T0B 2N0)
**Site Host / Partner:** Louis Bull Tribe (Maskwacis Cree Nation)
**ZEVIP Funding Tier Requested:** Up to 75% (Indigenous-led or partnered infrastructure)

---

## 1. Project Overview & Strategic Alignment

Renvolt is a Canadian EV infrastructure startup proposing the immediate deployment of a high-power, battery-backed DC fast-charging (DCFC) hub at the Bear Hills Casino & Travel Resort, located directly on the Queen Elizabeth II (QE2) Highway between Edmonton and Calgary, Alberta.

This project directly addresses a critical gap in Canada’s zero-emission vehicle infrastructure: the lack of high-speed charging options in rural and Indigenous communities along major transportation corridors. By partnering with the Louis Bull Tribe, Renvolt will deploy a "Scalable 2-Stall" dynamic power-sharing architecture that delivers premium 150kW fast-charging capabilities while overcoming the severe grid constraints that typically prevent rural deployments.

The project fulfills all core ZEVIP mandates by introducing public, highly visible DCFC infrastructure in an underserved priority zone, while simultaneously creating a new, passive revenue stream for an Indigenous-owned enterprise.

## 2. Technical Feasibility & Innovation (The Battery-Backed Solution)

The primary barrier to deploying DCFC infrastructure in rural Alberta is the exorbitant cost and timeline associated with utility service upgrades. Traditional 150kW fast chargers demand massive, unpredictable spikes from the grid, triggering 6-12 month delays and $100,000+ upgrade fees from utilities like FortisAlberta.

**The Renvolt Solution:**
This project utilizes a proprietary behind-the-meter architecture to completely eliminate grid impact. The site will feature:

* Two (2) DCFC Dispenser Posts (CCS1 and NACS compatible).
* One (1) 150kW Central Power Cabinet with Dynamic Power Sharing.
* One (1) 100kWh / 50kW Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) Battery Energy Storage System (BESS).

**How it Works (Zero-Export Grid Limit):**
Instead of hitting the grid with a massive 150kW load during a charging session, the Renvolt hub operates as a predictable, flat 50kW commercial load. The 100kWh BESS trickle-charges continuously from the utility grid at a maximum of 50kW. When an EV plugs in, the battery rapidly discharges at 150kW into the vehicle.

This decoupling of the grid tie from the dispensing speed ensures the site does not require a complex Generator Interconnection Application (GIA) or expensive substation upgrades. Furthermore, the dynamic 150kW cabinet strictly complies with ZEVIP's requirement that at least one connector be capable of delivering 100kW or more, while safely dropping to 75kW per vehicle when both stalls are occupied.

## 3. Financial Viability & Capital Efficiency

Renvolt’s battery-buffered architecture radically reduces total Capital Expenditure (CapEx) compared to traditional fast-charging deployments.

**CapEx Efficiency:**
By avoiding massive utility trenching, new medium-voltage transformers, and complex civil works, the total estimated project cost for this high-performance hub is **$207,300 CAD**. This lean footprint allows federal grant dollars to stretch significantly further, subsidizing a scalable model that can be replicated across rural Canada.

**OpEx Viability (Demand Charge Elimination):**
Traditional DCFC operators in Alberta face crippling monthly "demand charges" based on their highest 15-minute spike of electricity usage (often exceeding $2,000/month in standby costs alone). Because the Renvolt battery strictly limits grid draw to 50kW, demand charges are algorithmically slashed by over 66%, ensuring the site remains profitable even during the low-utilization early years of regional EV adoption.

## 4. Community Impact & Indigenous Partnership

The Bear Hills Casino is uniquely positioned as an economic anchor for the Louis Bull Tribe and the broader Maskwacis area.

**Economic Capture:**
Unlike standard gas-station rollouts where EV drivers buy coffee and leave, this deployment turns the 30-to-45 minute EV charging session into a strategic asset for the host. The infrastructure forcefully pulls high-income, intercity EV traffic off the QE2 highway and into the casino property, driving immense secondary economic activity for the Louis Bull Tribe's restaurants, hotel, and gaming floors.

**Revenue Sharing:**
The project is built on an equitable, zero-cost model for the host. The Louis Bull Tribe will incur $0 in capital or maintenance costs but will receive a monthly passive royalty from gross charging revenue, turning an underutilized section of parking-lot asphalt into a green energy asset.

**Scalability:**
This 2-stall pilot is designed to prove the economics of battery-backed rural charging. Once utilization reaches 20% capacity, the power electronics are modularly upgradeable, allowing Renvolt and the Louis Bull Tribe to easily scale the site to 4 or 6 stalls as national EV adoption accelerates toward the 2035 federal mandates.

3. **Procurement:** Received initial vendor quotes for NEMA 3R, winter-rated (-40°C) equipment.
