## What Is ZEVIP?

The **Zero-Emission Vehicle Infrastructure Program** is a federal program run by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan). It funds the deployment of EV charging infrastructure across Canada. The "Public Places" stream is the one relevant to RBS.

- **Portal:** [Transportation and Fuels Decarbonisation Programs](https://fuels-decarbonisation-carburants.canada.ca)
- **Program page:** [NRCan ZEVIP](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/transportation-alternative-fuels/zero-emission-vehicle-infrastructure-program/21876)
- **Planning Map:** [EV Charging Planning Map](https://app.geo.ca/en-ca/map-browser/record/fb7dd0e4-2feb-4fc1-96e7-aa626109f09c)

### RBS Pilot Site Calculation

| Your Setup | Funding |
|---|---|
| **4× 100 kW DC fast chargers** | 4 × $75,000 = **$300,000** |
| 2× Level 2 co-located connectors (optional) | 2 × $5,000 = $10,000 |
| **Maximum ZEVIP funding for this site** | **$300,000-$310,000** |
| **But capped at 50% of total project cost** | If CapEx = $200K → max grant = **$100,000** |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> ZEVIP covers up to **50% of total project costs** (75% for Indigenous-led projects). So even though per-charger caps are generous, your actual grant is capped at half your total CapEx.

### Expenses — What's Eligible and What's Not

| Eligible (ZEVIP will reimburse) | NOT Eligible |
|---|---|
| Charger hardware (purchase only) | Land purchase or lease costs |
| Battery storage hardware | Legal fees |
| Electrical installation | Ongoing electricity costs |
| Engineering and professional services | Networking/software subscription fees |
| Permits and licensing | Marketing costs |
| Environmental assessments | Salaries / operating costs |
| Shipping and delivery | Equipment leasing costs |

> [!WARNING]
> **Your battery co-location** is eligible as capital equipment. But your ongoing **energy management SaaS revenue** and **demand charge savings** are NOT grant-funded activities — they're your business model *after* deployment. Don't confuse the two in your application.

### CO₂ Impact Calculation

ZEVIP applications are scored partly on environmental impact. Here's a simple calculation:

```
Conservative assumptions:
- 4 chargers × 100 kW × 10% utilization × 24 hrs = 960 kWh/day
- Annual: 960 × 365 = 350,400 kWh/year
- Each kWh of EV charging displaces ~0.5 kg CO₂ (vs. gasoline equivalent)
- Annual CO₂ displaced: 350,400 × 0.5 = 175,200 kg = ~175 tonnes CO₂/year
- Over 10-year equipment life: ~1,750 tonnes CO₂
```

Use **175 tonnes CO₂/year** in your application. It's conservative and defensible.

The project will deploy four (4) 100 kW CCS-compatible DC fast chargers and a 100 kWh lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery system for demand charge optimization, serving the public 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
>
> Total project cost: $(amount). ZEVIP funding requested: $(50% of total).

### Section 2: Need & Gap Analysis (½ page)
> The proposed site is located in a Priority Zone ranked (4/5) on the NRCan EV Charging Planning Map, within (X) km of (highway/road name). The nearest existing DC fast charger is (X) km away at (location).
>
> Alberta currently has approximately (X) registered EVs with 15-20% annual growth projected. The (city) region has (X) existing public DC fast chargers, representing a charger-to-EV ratio of (X:1) — well below the recommended 1:10 ratio for adequate coverage.

### Section 3: Technical Approach (½ page)
> Equipment specifications:
> - Chargers: 4× (Brand/Model), 100 kW, CCS1/CCS2 connectors, OCPP 1.6J compliant, CSA/UL certified
> - Battery: 100 kWh LFP system, (Brand/Model), for peak demand shaving
> - Networking: (Platform name), cloud-monitored, payment-enabled
>
> The co-located battery system will charge during off-peak hours and discharge during peak demand, reducing grid impact and operating costs while ensuring reliable fast-charging service.

### Section 4: Project Timeline (¼ page)

| Phase | Timeline | Milestone |
|---|---|---|
| Agreement signing | Month 0 | ZEVIP agreement executed |
| Site preparation | Months 1-3 | Electrical upgrade, permits, concrete pad |
| Equipment procurement | Months 2-4 | Chargers + battery ordered and delivered |
| Installation + commissioning | Months 4-6 | Equipment installed, tested, connected to grid |
| Public launch | Month 6 | Site open 24/7, fully operational |

### Section 5: Sustainability & Impact (¼ page)
> Estimated annual CO₂ displacement: 175 tonnes (conservative, based on 10% utilization)
> Over 10-year equipment life: 1,750 tonnes CO₂
>
> RBS is committed to the long-term operation and maintenance of this site. Revenue from charging fees and energy optimization ensures financial sustainability beyond the ZEVIP funding period.
