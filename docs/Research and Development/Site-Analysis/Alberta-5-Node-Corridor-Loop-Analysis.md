## The Complete 5-Node Loop

Using the NRCan ZEVIP priority map as our guide, this loop targets every **Rank 4** corridor gap in Central-Northern Alberta, creating an interlocking ring that an EV can drive completely, with no leg exceeding 160 km between DCFC hubs.

| Leg | From | To | Distance | Highway | NRCan Rank |
|-----|------|----|----------|---------|------------|
| 1 | Bear Hills (Maskwacis) | Ponoka | 60 km | QE2 | Rank 4-5 |
| 2 | Ponoka | Drayton Valley | 158 km | Hwy 22 South | Rank 3-4 |
| 3 | Drayton Valley | Slave Lake | 165 km | Hwy 22 North | Rank 4 |
| 4 | Slave Lake | Lac La Biche | 175 km* | Hwy 2/18/63 | Rank 3-4 |
| 5 | Lac La Biche | Bear Hills (Edmonton) | 215 km | Hwy 63/2 QE2 | Rank 3-4 |

> *Note: Leg 4 (Slave Lake → Lac La Biche) at 175km is the ONE leg that exceeds a comfortable 150km range. This is flagged as a future mid-corridor opportunity near Barrhead/Westlock if the loop is tightened to be "100% range-anxiety-free."

## Per-Node Analysis

---

### 🟢 NODE 1: Bear Hills Casino (Maskwacis) — Phase 1 ACTIVE

**Site:** Bear Hills Casino & Travel Resort, QE2 / Hwy 611
**ZEVIP Priority Rank:** 4–5 ✅
**Traffic Volume:** ~35,000 vehicles/day (QE2 is Alberta's busiest highway)
**Charging Gap:** 130 km between Edmonton and Red Deer, no reliable DCFC

**Competing Chargers within 30 km:**

| Network | Speed | Stalls | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| None | — | — | No DCFC within 60 km radius |

**Verdict: ✅ GO — Phase 1. Uncontested. Maximum ZEVIP score.**
Louis Bull Tribe ownership = 75% ZEVIP Indigenous tier. Zero competition. Contact confirmed: Roger Anderson (GM), Gordon Rauscher (President). LOI drafted and ready.

---

### 🟢 NODE 2: Ponoka (QE2 Chain Completion) — Phase 3

**Site:** Ponoka Travel Centre, 4902 50th St (Hwy 2 interchange)
**ZEVIP Priority Rank:** 3–4 ✅
**Traffic Volume:** ~30,000–35,000 vehicles/day (QE2)
**Charging Gap:** Completes the Bear Hills → Ponoka → Lacombe/Red Deer QE2 fast-charge chain. Without Ponoka, there is a 95 km gap north of Lacombe.

**Competing Chargers within 30 km:**

| Network | Speed | Stalls | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| Legacy Ford Ponoka | Level 2 (FLO) | 1 | Operational — negligible |
| Lacombe SureCharge | 180 kW | 4+ | 15 km south — different exit |
| Lacombe Tesla | 250 kW | 8 | 15 km south — different exit |

**Analysis:** Lacombe is saturated. BUT Ponoka itself has zero fast charging. Northbound QE2 drivers coming from Bear Hills will pass Ponoka before Lacombe. This site captures the traffic that doesn't want to detour south.

**Verdict: ✅ GO — Phase 3. Ponoka exit is uncontested. Lacombe competition is irrelevant at a different exit.**
ZEVIP tier: 50% standard (75% possible if Samson Cree Nation/Maskwacis partnership is extended from Bear Hills). Payback: ~3.8 years at 50%, ~1.9 years at 75%.

---

### 🟢 NODE 3: Slave Lake, Hwy 2 North — Phase 2A (Priority #1 Expansion)

**Site:** Sawridge Travel Centre, 600 Hwy 88 North (directly on Sawridge First Nation IR 150G)
**ZEVIP Priority Rank:** 4 ✅
**Traffic Volume:** ~5,650–6,130 vehicles/day (Hwy 2 North, AADT 2023 Alberta Transportation)
**Charging Gap:** 130 km (Edmonton → High Prairie). Zero reliable DCFC in the entire corridor.

**Competing Chargers within 50 km:**

| Network | Speed | Stalls | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| Slave Lake Municipal (Rec Centre) | 2× 100 kW | 2 (FLO) | Operational — limited hours, municipality pricing |
| No Tesla | — | — | None announced |
| No SureCharge | — | — | Not in Phase 1 or 2 rollout |

**Analysis:** The rec centre chargers are 100 kW FLO units (Level 3-lite) with restricted public hours and municipal pricing. They are not a real DCFC hub and are not on the Sawridge FN site. Our 4-stall 150 kW pull-through hub at the Sawridge Travel Centre (24/7, truck-friendly) is categorically different.

**Verdict: ✅ GO — Phase 2A. True Blue Ocean. No credible competition.**
Sawridge Travel Centre is ON Sawridge First Nation reserve land = automatic 75% ZEVIP Indigenous tier. No utility trench needed — the travel centre has 3-phase commercial service. This is the highest-value Phase 2 target in the entire pipeline.

---

### 🟢 NODE 4: Lac La Biche, Hwy 63 South — Phase 2B

**Site:** Britton's Your Independent Grocer (10527 101 Ave) **OR** 881 Corner Gas (Hwy 881/Lakeland Dr)
**ZEVIP Priority Rank:** 3 ✅
**Traffic Volume:** ~3,000–3,350 vehicles/day on Hwy 63 south segment (AADT 2023, Alberta Transportation)
**Charging Gap:** 80 km between Grassland (new 180 kW ChargeLab) and Fort McMurray corridor. Only Level 2 or sub-30 kW options currently in Lac La Biche itself.

**Competing Chargers within 50 km:**

| Network | Speed | Stalls | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| Tarrabain Dodge | Level 2 | 1 | Operational — dealer hours only |
| Swift Charge | 20.8 kW | 1 | Operational — slower than most Level 2 |
| County Grant (planned) | 2× 50 kW | 2 | Municipal, 2026–2029 window, compliance-grade |

**Analysis:** Lac La Biche has no real DCFC. The 20.8 kW Swift Charge is barely faster than a Tesla home wall connector. The county's planned 50 kW compliance chargers are 2+ years away and would still be undersized. A 150 kW Renvolt hub would be the only real fast charging option in a 175 km radius.

> ⚠️ **Site Selection Note:** The 881 Corner Gas on Lakeland Drive is our **preferred site** over the IGA because it sits directly on the provincial highway approach, capturing through-traffic before they reach town. The IGA is better for local dwell-time charging. **We should pursue both and let the site visit decide.**

**Verdict: ✅ GO — Phase 2B. Effectively zero competition.**
Beaver Lake Cree Nation (Treaty 6) reserve is adjacent to Lac La Biche town = 75% ZEVIP Indigenous partnership pathway. Timeline: 7–9 months. Payback: ~22 months.

---

### 🟢 NODE 5: Drayton Valley, Hwy 22 — Phase 3B

**Site:** Walmart Supercentre, 5217 Power Center Blvd (Hwy 22 corridor)
**ZEVIP Priority Rank:** 3–4 ✅
**Traffic Volume:** ~8,000–12,000 vehicles/day (Hwy 22, estimate based on Alberta Transportation Hwy 22 segments; Drayton Valley is the primary commercial center)
**Charging Gap:** 95 km (Leduc/Devon to Rocky Mountain House). Zero DCFC of any kind on Hwy 22 corridor.

**Competing Chargers within 50 km:**

| Network | Speed | Stalls | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| None | — | — | **Absolutely nothing. Zero. Blank.** |

**Analysis:** Drayton Valley is an industrial hub at the junction of Hwy 22 (north-south) and Hwy 39 (east-west), serving the oil patch workforce. F-150 Lightnings and Silverado EVs are the exact demographic here. Power Center Blvd (Walmart, Canadian Tire, Tim Hortons) has massive parking, 3-phase commercial infrastructure already in the pad, and a captive audience who will shop for 45 minutes while charging.

> ⚠️ **Key Risk:** O'Chiese First Nation is 80 km west — borderline for ZEVIP 75% Indigenous eligibility. ZEVIP will likely approve this as a 50% standard corridor site but a partnership with O'Chiese FN needs to be formalized in writing to confirm the 75% tier. **Don't assume — confirm.**

**Verdict: ✅ GO — Phase 3B. Zero competition on the most trafficked rural industrial highway in Alberta.**

## The "Loop Closer" Node: Barrhead / Westlock (Phase 4+)

**Issue:** Leg 4 (Slave Lake → Lac La Biche, 175 km) slightly exceeds the 150 km radius comfortable range for many EVs, especially in winter at –30°C (where cold-weather range loss of 20-25% can reduce a 350 km range vehicle to ~260 km).

**Recommended Mid-Point:** Barrhead, AB (Hwy 18, roughly equidistant between Slave Lake and Lac La Biche).

- **Current DCFC:** Barrhead Ford 24 kW "DCFC" (practically unusable — requires dealer assistance, business hours only).
- **Verdict:** If EV adoption accelerates past 2028, Barrhead becomes the obvious fill-in node to make the loop 100% range-anxiety-free. **Leave on the Phase 4+ watchlist.**
